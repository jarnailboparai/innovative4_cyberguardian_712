The term of Invisible Key logger may refer to the name of a specific program, or it may simply refer to any software that operates without the user knowing, as in that sense it is essentially an invisible.

For a computer user is being monitored in secret, then it is easy to see why the person doing the monitoring would want to use a program which is running without the users knowledge. After all, if you are trying to catch someone doing something they shouldn't be doing, it's best if they do not know they are being observed!

When it comes to espionage and spying, stealth and invisibility is the name of the game, much as the stealth bomber sneaks into enemy territory unnoticed.

The one exception to using an invisible program is if someone is using it on themselves. For example, some people may run them on their own computers to help them save work should their system or program crash before they had a chance to save their work or send an email or whatever they were working on. Have you ever written a long email and then when you hit send you get taken to some error page, and then when you click back your email is gone? If you were running a monitoring program in this situation you could just go retrieve everything you typed and save yourself the time of having to remember everything you wrote and type it again.
